Question 1: How does branching enable significantly more complex program behavior?

By allowing for more possibilities/paths going into the end point through the use of a boolean.

Question 2: Why do you think the term consequent is used to refer to the statements that are executed when the Boolean test evaluates to true?

This is because a true boolean is the direct/expected result, or the consequent.

Section 2

Question 1: Why is the first Jump instruction conditional? What determines whether the jump will be executed or not?

The first jump is conditional because it is comparing the numbers 5 and 4 to see which is greater than the other. Whether the jump is executed or not is dependent on if the original number (5 in this case) is greater than 4 or not.

Question 2: What is the purpose of the Jump instruction after the consequent?

The purpose of the jump instruction after the consequent is to skip the alternative section and get to the afterAlternative section of the code.

Question 3: Why is there no need for a corresponding Jump instruction after the alternative?

No jump instruction is required after the alternative because it will flow into the afterAlternative section without a jump, and it will not randomly go back into the consequent section. Put simply, the code will run as intended without a jump instruction.
